Research Project: ESP32 Smart Obstacle Avoiding System

During my time as Vice President of the Robotics Club at Oxford International School (2022-2023), I led the research and development of an autonomous navigation unit. The project focused on building a Smart Obstacle Avoiding System powered by the ESP32 microcontroller.

Technical Implementation

The core objective was to create a robot capable of real-time spatial awareness. I coordinated group projects where we explored how the ESP32’s processing power could be used to interpret sensor data and trigger immediate motor responses. We focused on:

  • Sensor Integration: Implementing hardware that could detect physical barriers within a specific range
  • Autonomous Logic: Developing the decision-making framework that allowed the system to navigate complex environments without human intervention
  • Hardware Precision: Ensuring the wiring and component placement met the rigorous standards required for mobile robotics
