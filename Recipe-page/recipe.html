Haleem

Haleem is a traditional dish that is popular in many parts of the world, particularly in the Middle East, South Asia, and Central Asia. It is a hearty and flavorful stew made with a combination of meat (usually beef, lamb, or chicken), lentils, wheat, and various spices. The ingredients are slow-cooked together for several hours until they break down and create a thick, porridge-like consistency. Haleem is often enjoyed during special occasions and festivals, and it is known for its rich taste and comforting qualities.

Ingredients Instructions
  • 1 cup wheat
  • 1 cup lentils
  • 500g meat (beef, lamb, or chicken)
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger paste
  • 2 teaspoons cumin powder
  • 2 teaspoons coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water or broth
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  1. Rinse soak grains
  2. Sauté chopped onions
  3. garlic ginger
  4. Cook meat browned
  5. add spices salt
  6. Add soaked grains
  7. Add water broth
  8. Simmer stir occasionally
  9. Garnish serve hot
